    Card play¶

    To play a card when it it is your turn, click on the card that you select (the system will force you to follow suit if you can).

    When it is you turn to play a yellow bar will appear next to the relevant hand.

    The trick cards will be displayed in the centre of the screen.

    Solo¶

    In Solo mode you will play declarer’s and and dummy’s if declarer is N or S. If declarer is E or W, you will play you own hand.

    The system will play E’s and W’s cards and S’s cards if your are in Solo mode and E or W is declarer.

    Duo¶

    In Duo mode, you will play declarer’s cards and dummy’s if you are declarer. If your partner is declarer the they will play declarer’s and dummy’s. If E or W is declarer, the you will play your hand and your partner theirs.

    Previous tricks¶

    If you wish to see which cards were played on previous tricks, click on Previous trick in the Menu. You can step backwards and forwards through all of the played tricks.

    Display auction¶

    You may remind yourself of the auction by clicking on Display auction int he Menu.

    Restart, Replay and Undo¶

    At any stage you may restart the board by clicking on Restart board in the Menu. This will restore all of the hands, remove all bids and restart the board.

    If you click on Replay in the Menu, the hands are restored but the bidding and auction remain intact.

    If you want to undo the last played card, click on Undo in the Menu.

    Makeable tricks¶

    You may see the makeable tricks for this board by clicking on Makeable tricks in the Menu. (Uses a Double-Dummy solver.)

    Claim/Concede¶

    If you wish to claim or concede a certain number of tricks before you have finished playing, you may click on Claim/Concede on the Menu. This will show the claim/concede dialog

    Select the number of tricks that you wish to claim or concede. The system will check your claim and either accept

    or reject the claim.

    Final score¶

    When all of the cards have been played, the system will display the final score.

    If you click on Compare score the system will compare the score that you achieved with the score that it would have made had it played the hand. (Note this just simulates play and does is not necessarily match the score given in Makeable tricks.)
